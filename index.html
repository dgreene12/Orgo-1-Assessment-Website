Organic Chemistry I Help Rooms
Program Assessment

Introduction

  • Students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds, despite initial interest, leave STEM fields more often than their wealthier peers due to financial and academic pressures.

  • "Help room" programs offer drop-in tutoring but may be less accessible to low-income students due to limited availability and work schedules.

  • Examining the impact of "help rooms" is key to helping low-income students succeed in pre-health tracks and reducing their underrepresentation in medicine.

Research Question

  • To what extent is student participation in help room programs for Organic Chemistry I associated with a student’s grade in and completion of the course?

Objectives

Objective 1:

Better understand help-seeking behavior

Objective 2:

Support retention in STEM fields for all students

Objective 3:

Improve support initiatives to foster inclusion

Conceptual Framework

  • This approach combines psychological, behavioral, and social factors to understand how learning programs affect STEM student success at R1 institutions.

  • It aims to create insights for designing and applying academic support strategies that are motivating and inclusive for all students.

  • The extent of students’ learning and development is directly proportional to the quantity and quality of their involvement in the educational experience

  • Environment influences students’ understanding of Organic Chemistry material, their satisfaction with the course, their overall academic achievement, and their persistence in STEM fields

  • Three-Pronged Approach: competence, autonomy, and relatedness

  • Intrinsic motivation arises when students’ needs for competence, autonomy, and relatedness are met

  • Knowledge is constructed through social interactions

  • Cultural and social contexts shape learning experiences

  • Students are exposed to a variety of viewpoints and problem-solving approaches in help rooms, enriching their understanding of the subject matter and fostering critical thinking and adaptability

Methodology

  • The study will complement quantitative analysis with qualitative methods to capture the intricacies of the students’ experiences with the help room.

Sample

  • The primary subjects for this study will be undergraduate students enrolled in Organic Chemistry I during the period of investigation.

  • This study will adopt a total population sampling approach to include all students enrolled in Organic Chemistry I.

  • Students who drop the Organic Chemistry I before the mid-semester mark will be excluded from the final analysis to ensure that the results reflect
    help room engagement patterns over the entire semester.

Data Collection

Knowledge Tests

  • Examine students’ proficiency and conceptual understanding through tailored formative assessments, specifically midterm and final exams

  • Seek to identify any perceived barriers to accessing the help room and capture students’ self-regulatory learning strategies

Surveys

  • Administered at beginning and end of course to students who frequent the help room

  • Serve as a vital tool for identifying and understanding the students’ learning strategies, coping mechanisms, and overall engagement with the course content

Reflections

  • Reflective prompts integrated into the periodic intake form and surveys

  • Guiding questions aim to collect structured insights while also accommodating personal narratives

  • Crafted to align with contemporary theories of metacognition and self-regulated learning

Data Analysis

Spearman Correlation

  • Particularly suited for analyzing data such as categorizing help room visits into distinct frequency bins (e.g., 1-3 visits, 4-6 visits, etc.)

  • Allows researcher to effectively identify potential non-linear relationships between the categorized frequency of help room visits and students' grades

  • α = 0.05

Linear Regression

  • Assess the continuous relationship between help room attendance and academic performance

  • Allows for a nuanced understanding of how changes in the number of help room visits might correlate with variations in students’ grades, controlling for other potential confounding variables

  • α = 0.05

Qualitative Analysis

  • Narratives explore the underlying reasons behind the statistical relationships, such as how help room visits impact students’ confidence and mastery of Organic Chemistry I concepts.

  • Coded using NVivo

Discussion

  • The findings could provide crucial insights into enhancing teaching methodologies and student engagement strategies

  • Demonstrating the effectiveness of STEM education support models could attract further investments in similar initiatives, benefitting universities, faculty, students, and the community

  • Future studies should examine the students who did not attend help room program. This will help eliminate survivorship bias.
